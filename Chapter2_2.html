Subspace, Dimension, Rank

What is subspace ?

A subspace in Rn is a set H in Rn and has the following three properties:

  1. 1. The zero vector is a member of H;
  2. 2. For any vector u and v in H, u+v belongs to H;
  3. 3. For any vector u and real Numbers in H, cu belongs to H.

That is, the subspace is closed for addition and scalar multiplication. For example, the plane through the origin is a very typical subspace.

The basis of a subspace

A subspace generally contains an infinite number of vectors, problems in a subspace are best solved by studying a small finite set that can generate the subspace. A basis for H in Rn is A linearly independent set in H, which generates H, and the parameter vectors for the solution set Ax is equal to 0 are essentially the basis for Null A.

{e1 and e2... en} is called the standard basis for Rn.

Dimension of a subspace

The dimension of a non-null subspace H is the number of vectors of any basis of H, denoting dim H. The dimension of the null subspace is defined as zero.

Rn has dimensions of n, and each basis of Rn is made up of n vectors, and in R3 a plane that goes through the origin is two-dimensional, and a line that goes through the origin is one-dimensional.

Rank of matrix:

The rank of matrix A is the dimension of the column space of A, which is called rank A. Since the pivot column of A forms A basis of Col A, the rank of A is exactly the number of pivot column of A after simplifying A to row ladder type.

Theorem of rank and invertible matrix

Assuming that A is an n-by-n matrix, each of the following statements is equivalent to saying that A is invertible.

  1. 1. The column vectors of A form A basis for Rn;
  2. 2. Col A=Rn;
  3. 3. dim Col A=n;
  4. 4. Rank A=n;
  5. 5. Null A= {0};
  6. 6. dim Null A=0.
